# Spelklar — Product Vision

## North Star

Spelklar exists to eliminate the administrative burden of grassroots sports in Sweden. Volunteers — mostly parents — are the most underserved users in the sports ecosystem. By owning the 60 minutes of the match with an ultra-simple interface, we capture the high-value data needed to automate club administration and unlock local sponsorship revenue.

---

## Where We Are Now (Phase 1 — Live)

The core Match Engine is built and running. A volunteer at the Sekretariat can:

- Load a match with a dynamically synced roster (CSV import)
- Record standardised events: Goals, Penalties (2/5/10 min), Timeouts, and Period ends
- Follow a live Supporter Feed in real time via the companion view
- Administer matches through an operator dashboard

**The immediate value:** a paperless, real-time match protocol that replaces the clipboard — with zero training required.

---

## Phase 2 — Monetisation

Once the match protocol is trusted by clubs, we activate three layered revenue streams. Each has a different buyer and a different pricing mechanism, so they compound rather than compete.

### Stream 1 — Performance Notifications (Buyer: the Club)

Spelklar sells contextual push notifications to the club, timed to match events (warm-up, goals, final whistle). The club uses them to drive Kiosk sales and reinforce the supporter experience.

**Example notifications:**
- *"Your team plays in 10 minutes! Don't forget to grab snacks from the Kiosk."*
- *"Goal! Celebrate with 10% off your next coffee at the Kiosk."*
- *"The match has ended! Thank you for supporting [Team Name]!"*

**Pricing lever:** Move from a flat channel fee to **per-redemption revenue sharing** (Spelklar takes a % of each Kiosk sale that originates from a notification). This aligns incentives and scales with club activity.

### Stream 2 — Local Sponsor Marketplace (Buyer: the Local Business)

Local businesses — the café next door, the sports shop in town — buy a **"Match Sponsor" slot** directly through Spelklar, without going through the club. Spelklar delivers the branded notification and takes a direct margin.

**Example notifications:**
- *"This match is sponsored by [Sponsor]. Show this message for 10% off at [Sponsor]."*

This is effectively **hyperlocal advertising**, priced per match or per season. The club gets visibility, the sponsor gets footfall, Spelklar earns without touching a kr of Kiosk revenue.

## Phase 3 — Additional Revenue Streams (Club & Supporters)

These streams go beyond notifications and build a richer economic layer on top of the match-day data Spelklar already captures.

### Stream 3 — Kiosk Pre-Orders (Buyer: the Supporter)

Supporters pre-order Kiosk items — coffee, hot dogs, snacks — directly through the Supporter view before they arrive at the arena. The club serves more volume with less queue pressure. Spelklar takes a small transaction fee per order.

**Value prop to the club:** Higher Kiosk throughput per match with the same number of volunteers.

### Stream 4 — Digital Lottery / Tombola (Buyer: the Supporter)

Swedish clubs commonly run tombolas. Spelklar makes this instant and in-app — *"Guess the final score and win a prize"* — triggered at the start of the second period. Supporters pay 10–20 kr via Swish; the club keeps the pot minus a Spelklar platform cut.

**Value prop to the club:** A zero-effort fundraising activation that runs automatically mid-match.

### Stream 5 — In-Match Digital Programme (Buyer: the Local Sponsor)

Replace the paper match programme with a digital version served inside the Supporter view. Local sponsors pay for placement — a banner, a profile, a discount code. The club earns the sponsorship fee with zero printing costs.

**Value prop to the club:** Sponsorship revenue with no manual work and no paper waste.

### Stream 6 — Micro-Fundraising Pushes (Buyer: the Supporter)

Immediately after a win (highest emotional engagement of the match), push a single-tap donation prompt: *"Help us buy new nets — 50 kr via Swish."* Contextual, frictionless, and far more effective than a newsletter appeal. Club keeps 100% of funds; Spelklar earns by enabling the moment.

**Value prop to the club:** Converts emotional peaks into direct income for equipment and travel.

### Stream 7 — Memory Packages (Buyer: the Parent)

After a match, offer parents a digital photo or highlight reel package. A club photographer links their content to Spelklar; parents receive a post-match notification and pay to download. Spelklar facilitates the transaction and takes a platform fee.

**Value prop to the club:** A new revenue stream from existing match-day infrastructure with no extra volunteer effort.

### Stream 8 — Fan Pass Subscription (Buyer: the Supporter)

Supporters pay a small seasonal fee (e.g. 99 kr/season) for premium access to the Supporter view — richer live stats, early lineup announcements, end-of-season highlights. Club earns recurring income; Spelklar takes a platform cut.

**Value prop to the club:** Predictable off-season income from their most engaged supporters.

### Stream 9 — Membership Renewal Reminders (Buyer: the Club)

The platform knows exactly when supporter engagement peaks: match days. Spelklar triggers membership renewal prompts at that precise moment rather than a generic September email. Higher conversion rates mean more annual membership revenue for the club, for which Spelklar charges an activation fee.

**Value prop to the club:** The right message to the right person at the moment they care most about their club.

### Stream 10 — Club Reporting Subscription (Buyer: the Club Treasurer)

The match data Spelklar captures — attendance, event frequency, supporter engagement — is exactly what clubs need for LOK-stöd applications and board reporting. Packaging this as a **monthly "Club Dashboard" subscription** gives clubs a recurring, tangible reason to pay independently of any given match.

**Value prop to the Treasurer:** *"Your LOK-stöd report is already filled in. Export and submit."*

## Phase 4 — The Data Bridge

We do not aim to replace the club's ERP system (SportAdmin, Profixio, laget.se). We aim to become its most trusted Data Provider.

- **Headless Integration:** Spelklar provides the match-day Frontend; after the final whistle it pushes the verified Result to the incumbent system automatically.
- **LOK-stöd Automation:** The structured event data we capture is exactly what clubs need to file for state/municipal activity grants — removing hours of manual reporting per season.

This is how we win the board's approval: we save the Treasurer time and make money for the Club.

---

## Market Environment (Sweden 2026)

- **The Transition Gap:** 2026 is the year of the Idrottsarenan migration. Clubs are confused and need a bridge to the new national system — Spelklar is that bridge.
- **The Swish Standard:** In Sweden, "Digital Payment" means Swish; "Digital ID" means BankID/Freja+. These are infrastructure requirements, not features.
- **The Förening Culture:** Club decisions are made by democratically elected boards. Our pitch must prove we save time for the Treasurer and make money for the Club.

## Key Terminology (Spelklar Glossary)

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Sekretariat** | The volunteer-led scoring table at a match |
| **Förening** | The Association / Club legal structure |
| **Domare** | The Referee (who often co-signs the digital protocol) |
| **Kiosk** | The match-day café, typically run by parents via Swish |
| **LOK-stöd** | State/municipal activity grants — the financial lifeblood of Swedish clubs |
